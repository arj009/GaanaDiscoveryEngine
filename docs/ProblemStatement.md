# Problem Statement: Enhancing Music Discovery at Gaana

## Role Context
You are a Product Manager on the Growth Team at Gaana.

## The Challenge
Gaana has successfully acquired millions of users and developed one of the world's most sophisticated music recommendation systems. Despite these technical achievements, a significant proportion of user listening time is still dominated by repetitive behaviors—such as replaying familiar playlists, sticking to known artists, and looping previously discovered tracks. 

One of the company’s primary strategic objectives is to **increase meaningful music discovery** and **reduce repetitive listening habits**, thereby deepening user engagement and broadening content consumption.

## Part 1: Build an AI-Powered User Feedback & Discovery Engine
To solve this, we must first deeply understand the user's current friction points with music discovery. The goal is to build an AI-powered system to aggregate and analyze qualitative user feedback from multiple channels, including:
- App Store & Google Play Store reviews
- Reddit discussions & community forums
- Social media conversations

### Key Questions to Answer
This review analysis system must be designed to uncover actionable product insights by answering the following questions:
1. **Discovery Friction:** Why do users struggle to discover new music?
2. **Recommendation Pitfalls:** What are the most common frustrations with our current recommendation engine?
3. **User Intent:** What specific listening behaviors or moods are users trying to achieve when seeking new music?
4. **Repetition Drivers:** What psychological or UX factors cause users to repeatedly listen to the same content?
5. **Segment Analysis:** Which user segments (e.g., casual listeners vs. audiophiles) experience distinct discovery challenges?
6. **Unmet Needs:** What latent or unmet needs consistently emerge across different feedback channels?
